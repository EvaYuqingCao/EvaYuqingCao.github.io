Eva Yuqing Cao

Eva Yuqing Cao is a multimedia storyteller, comparatist, and activist whose work crosses the border of the quantitative and the qualitative, converging art, literature linguistics and pressing social issues with computer-science oriented technologies. Her creativity in art and literature as well as technological skills (Python, VR/AR, Html/Css/JavaScript, C++ for Unreal Engine, Adobe Creative Suite, Flixel, Corel VideoStudio) leads to the goal of designing connective media devices, apps and even startups that could narrate human experiences and emotions ---technologies that encounter and solve human problems. With the experience of interviewing reporting the stories of migrant workers in New Delhi, the homeless in the Jungle (Ithaca’s tent city) during Covid and sexual assault survivors, Eva believes in the power of the human voice and storytelling. A “super-literature” is required, an avant-garde form of literature that’s close to connective media that uses technology to spread and amplify the human voice.

With the academic background in comparative literature and German studies (BA Cornell University 2020. PhD Northwestern University) and consistent, passionate endeavors in visual arts and creative writing, Eva’s research on Trauma, Humanism and Posthumanism, Linguistics and translation, Philosophies of systems and paradigms have provided her the foundation to work on human-oriented technologies.
